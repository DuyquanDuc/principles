Principles

01

I AM OPTIMISTIC

Hope is motivation's fuel. Believe in the best is yet to come.

In Practice
  • Next year, I will have passed N1.
  • Next year, I will have a new job that pays better.
  • Next year, my start-up will succeed and make a decent amount of money.
  • Next year, I will have a consistent morning and night routine.
  • Next year, I will have perfected my set of principles.
02

I AM EARLY

Respect Through Time. If I am not early, I am late. Time reflects respect.

In Practice
  • Be 10 minutes early for every commitment.
  • The best time is 10 minutes ago, the next best is now.
  • The earlier, the better — do things early in the day, week, month, and year.
03

I DO NOT TRADE LONG-TERM HEALTH FOR SHORT-TERM COMFORT

Health is Foundation. My body is the base of everything I build.

In Practice
  • Choose the stairs over the elevator.
  • Choose doing nothing over doomscrolling when resting.
  • Choose the book over the phone for recreation.
04

I SHOW UP EVERY DAY

Consistency Over Intensity. Intensity is optional. Consistency is not.

In Practice
  • Always show up, even when you don't feel like it.
  • Try to do just 5 more minutes once you've started.
05

I DO NOT CREATE UNNECESSARY CONFLICT

Move in Harmony. I move with people, not against them.

In Practice
  • I speak calmly and softly. I let my actions do the talking.
  • I listen more than I speak. Avoid badmouthing, judging, and gossiping about others.
06

I CHOOSE THE HARD ROAD ON PURPOSE

Strength Through Response. Difficulty does not define me. My response does. I use hardship to grow. I choose the hard road on purpose, if it leads to growth.

In Practice
  • I will do it even when I'm anxious — that's the definition of courage.
07

I AM RESPONSIBLE FOR MY LIFE

Life is inherently unfair. I can choose to blame my parents, genetics, or circumstances, but I take extreme ownership of my choices and their consequences.

In Practice
  • I don't blame genetics, my parents, or my past. I shut up and do something about it.
08

I ANCHOR TO WHAT DOES NOT CHANGE

Anchor in the Permanent. Moods rise and fall. I hold onto what does not — my goals, the principles and the lessons I have earned.

In Practice
  • When the day is bad, return to the goals — they are the only thing the day cannot change.
  • Reread past lessons when emotion clouds judgement.

Be Bold, Start Cold.
